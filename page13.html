CHEAP VEGAN RAMEN

Just a Simple Bowl of a Hot Nutrient-Rich Ramen
to Wake You Up and Start a New Day.

FOR EACH BOWL, YOU'LL NEED:

1 block of Tofu, sliced into 6
1 small pack of Rat Ear Fungus or Tenga ng Daga in tagalog
2-3 cups of vegetable broth (to learn how, click here)
1 box of noodle
1/2 cup Carrots – finely chopped into matchsticks
1/8 cup Black Beans
Vegetable cooking oil

The Pulled Noodles

Ramen is a Japanese dish whose literally translation is "pulled noodles". It consists of Chinese wheat noodles served in a meat or fish-based broth that is often flavored with soy sauce or miso. It uses toppings such as sliced pork, nori, menma, and scallions.

Ramen had originated from China and was imported to Japan around the 17th and 20th century. In 1958, Momofuku Ando invents instant ramen after trial and error and establishes Nissin foods. “Chikin Ramen” became their first flavor. In 1971, Nissin invented Cup Noodles and it became popular throughout the world because of its effiency to use only hot water to cook.

But here, we are going to make a very cheap and easy hot vegan ramen!

  1. STEP 1 - Leave the rat ear fungus soaking in a bowl of water.

  2. STEP 2 -Heat vegetable oil on pan over medium heat. Fry the tofu until all sides are evenly crispy and light brown. Set it aside when done.
  3. STEP 2 - On a pot over high heat, pour vegetable broth. When it simmers, put the noodle and rat ear fungus then wait for the noodle to turn softer. (will usually take 1-2 minutes).
  4. STEP 3 - After 1-2, before the noodles come to a cook, put the carrot matchsticks and tofu and let the carrot soften a bit while the noodles come to a cook. After that, serve it in a bowl then enjoy your easy peasy hot vegan ramen!
