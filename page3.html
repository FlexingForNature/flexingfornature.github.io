VEGAN BIBIMBAP

The pride of Korean cuisine in Vegan form!

FOR EACH BOWL, YOU'LL NEED:

1 tsp olive oil or any vegetable oil
6 tbsp grinded garlic
1 tbsp soy sauce

To Be Soaked in Olive Oil/Sesame Oil with 1/2 tsp salt overnight:
1/2 cup Mushroom - stems removed, caps chopped lengthwise
1 cup Carrot - finely chopped into thin matchsticks
15-20 leaves Spinach - washed thoroughly

Other Ingredients:
1 cup Soy bean sprouts - boiled
1/2 cup Cucumber - finely chopped into matchsticks
1/2 cup Vegan Kimchi (click here to learn how)
1 cup rice

"THE PRIDE OF KOREAN CUISINE"

Bibimbap is from the words bibim and bap. Bibim means mixed ingredients while bap means rice. It existed centuries ago when rural Koreans learned how to recycle their vegetable leftovers and meat. Bibimbap is also rich in symbolism. Every ingredient of the dish pertains to heal a certain part of one's body.

There are several kinds of bibimbap:

Dolsot-Bibimbap is the usual way of serving bibimbap in restaurants worldwide. Dolsot means "hot stone pot". In this way, Bibimbap is served in a very hot stone pot and before arranging the ingredients on it, the bottom of the bowl is coated with sesame oil so that the rice would not stick but get crispier instead. A raw egg yolk is usually placed on the top of the rice.

Jeonju-Bibimbap uses rice that is cooked in beef broth instead of plain water. This is one of the most famous dishes in South Korea and around the word.

Yakcho Bibimbap uses certain medicinal herbs as one of the ingredients. It originated from the city of Jecheon which is the best place to grow herbs.

Hoedeopbap uses raw seafood such as tilapia, salmon, tuna and octupus instead of meat. The term hoe in the word means raw fish. It is popular in the coastal areas of Korea where fish are copious.

But this time, we're going to make vegan bibimbap where we'll replace meat, seafood and egg with mushroom and we'll make it as plant-based as possible.

  1. STEP 1 - Heat pan over medium heat, put 1 tsp of olive oil or any vegetable oil. Saute 1 tbsp grinded garlic. When the garlic turns golden brown, simmer 1 cup water then boil with 1/2 cup soy bean sprouts. Sprinkle with salt and boil for 5 minutes. Hang and put the soy bean sprouts in a bowl then put 1 tbsp sesame oil. Try it now!
  2. STEP 2 - Heat pan over medium heat, put 1 tsp of olive oil or any vegetable oil. Saute 1 tbsp grinded garlic. When the garlic turns golden brown, saute 1/2 cup mushroom along with it. Hang and put in a dish after 3 minutes.
  3. STEP 3 - Heat pan over medium heat, put 1 tsp of olive oil or any vegetable oil. Saute 1 tbsp grinded garlic. When the garlic turns golden brown, saute spinach along with it then put 1 tbsp sesame oil and sprinkle with sesame seeds. After 3 minutes, hang and put in the same dish beside mushroom.
  4. STEP 4 - Repeat step 3 but with carrot instead of spinach.

  5. STEP 5 - With big mushroom, bath the 2 halves with soy sauce, sauted 1 tsp garlic, sesame oil and sesame seeds then heat them all in a pan for 5 minutes. After that, fry the mushroom halves with 1 tsp of olive oil. With tofu, do the same process as big mushroom.
  6. STEP 6 - Put 1 cup rice in the center of your desired bowl. Put soy bean sprouts, mushroom, spinach, carrot, zuccini, cucumber and kimchi around rice. Top the rice with big mushroom or tofu. Try it now!
