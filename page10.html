Simple Buddha Bowl

A Simple Plant-Based Protein-Iron Bowl with Vitamin C for more effectivity!

INGREDIENTS

1/2 cup Squash - boiled
10 Sweet Potato Leaves - boiled
1/4 cup Soy Bean Sprouts - boiled

1 cube Tofu - cubed into 27 cubes
1 small can of Button Mushrooms - sliced into halves
1/4 cup Black Beans

2 Tomatoes - chopped
10 Chinese Celery Leaves
1 cup Rice

FOR TOFU SAUCE:
     > 30g Oyster Sauce
     > 1/2 tbsp Lemon/Calamansi Juice
     > 1 tbsp Honey

The Balanced meal

A Buddha bowl is a vegetarian meal. It is served on a single bowl or high-rimmed plate where small portions of several foods is served cold. These may include whole grains such as quinoa or brown rice, plant proteins such as chickpeas or tofu, and vegetables. It is named for its big, round Buddha belly shape.

It is said that the Buddha bowl started from Buddha, collecting different vegetable 

  1. STEP 1 - Boil the squash, soy bean sprouts and sweet potato leaves. You can also boil the squash with soy bean sprouts but seperately boil sweet potato leaves. Set aside when done.

  2. STEP 2 - Prepare the tofu sauce by mixing oyster sauce, lemon/calamansi juice, honey. Sprinkle some sesame seeds  if you have them.

  3. STEP 3 - Heat pan over medium heat, fry the tofu cubes until all sides are evenly crispy and light brown. Pour the tofu sauce, black beans and mushrooms. If the sauce tasted salty, pour small amounts of water until the taste gets balanced. Set aside when done.
  4. STEP 4 - Mix the chinese celery leaves and chopped tomatoes. Set aside when done.

  5. STEP 5 - Put the cup of rice in between of the bowl then put the Squash, soy bean sprouts, sweet potato leaves, tofu-mushroom-blackbeans, and tomato-chinese celery on the space around the rice. Enjoy your heavy and healthy bowl!
